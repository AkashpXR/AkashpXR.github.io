Advanced AI Simulation for VR Experience

I developed an AI traffic system framework to simulate a realistic and dynamic warehouse environment for operator training. This framework enhances immersion by introducing intelligent Material Handling Equipment (MHE)—such as forklifts and reach trucks—that autonomously navigate the environment, perform load pick-up and drop-off operations, and interact with the player in real-time.

Custom Hardware Integration for VR Simulators

I implemented custom hardware integration, including Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) support, to enhance the realism and tactile feedback of VR training simulators. This involved interfacing real-world input devices—such as joysticks, control levers, and hydraulic-style controls—with the virtual environment to accurately mimic the operation of actual machinery.

By developing custom input communication protocols, including BLE-based data transmission, I enabled seamless, low-latency synchronization between wireless physical hardware and virtual actions. This wireless integration allows for greater flexibility in simulator setups while maintaining high responsiveness and stability.

The system significantly improves immersion and bridges the gap between digital training and real-world equipment operation. It's especially effective for heavy machinery simulators like forklifts, cranes, and reach stackers, enabling operators to train using controls that closely replicate real-world interfaces—even in wireless configurations.
